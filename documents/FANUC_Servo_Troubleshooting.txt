# FANUC Servo Troubleshooting Guide

## Common Servo Alarms and Solutions

### SRVO-001: Servo motor overcurrent
**Cause**: Motor draws excessive current due to mechanical binding or motor fault.
**Solution**:
1. Check for mechanical obstruction
2. Verify motor connections
3. Check for motor insulation breakdown
4. Replace motor if necessary

### SRVO-002: Servo motor overload
**Cause**: Continuous operation at high load conditions.
**Solution**:
1. Reduce cycle time or load
2. Improve cooling
3. Check for proper lubrication

### SRVO-023: Servo motor overheat
**Cause**: Motor temperature exceeds safe operating limits.
**Solution**:
1. Check cooling fan operation
2. Clean ventilation passages
3. Reduce duty cycle
4. Verify ambient temperature

### SRVO-036: Servo parameter invalid
**Cause**: Incorrect or corrupted servo parameters.
**Solution**:
1. Reload default parameters
2. Check motor type selection
3. Verify encoder settings

### SRVO-050: VRDY signal off
**Cause**: Servo amplifier not ready.
**Solution**:
1. Check power supply voltage
2. Verify emergency stop circuit
3. Check amplifier status LED

### SRVO-062: BZAL alarm
**Cause**: Brake release signal abnormal.
**Solution**:
1. Check brake relay
2. Verify brake power supply
3. Inspect brake mechanism

### SRVO-063: Robot servo alarm
**Cause**: General servo communication error.
**Solution**:
1. Check servo amplifier connections
2. Verify encoder cable integrity
3. Reset and reinitialize
4. Check for electromagnetic interference

### SRVO-065: OHAL1 alarm
**Cause**: Servo amplifier overheat.
**Solution**:
1. Check cooling fan operation
2. Clean heat sink
3. Verify ambient temperature
4. Reduce duty cycle

### SRVO-068: DTERR alarm
**Cause**: Digital torque limit exceeded.
**Solution**:
1. Check for mechanical binding
2. Reduce acceleration/deceleration
3. Verify torque limit settings

## General Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Read alarm details**: Use MENU > ALARM > SHOW to see full alarm message
2. **Check history**: Review alarm history for patterns
3. **Power cycle**: Sometimes a controlled restart resolves transient issues
4. **Inspect cables**: Check all servo and encoder cables for damage
5. **Verify parameters**: Ensure servo parameters match motor specifications

## Important Safety Notes

- Always follow lockout/tagout procedures
- Wear appropriate PPE when working on robots
- Never bypass safety interlocks
- Consult FANUC documentation for specific procedures
